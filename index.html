The problem

The world's most broken supply chain.

When you donate clothes to a charity shop, the majority are shipped to Pakistan, India, or Africa, where thousands of people hand-sort every item, one by one, to figure out if it's worth sending back.

A Levi's jacket worth £50 leaves for pennies. The supply chain ships your clothes around the world twice because the technology to sort them locally has never existed. Until now.

That double journey is where the carbon comes from. Items that turn out to be worthless on arrival get burned or landfilled, having already crossed an ocean. Fleek sorts at the point of collection: a phone photo, six seconds, a grade and a price. Only what's worth moving gets moved. The supply chain shrinks. The emissions shrink with it.
